Router-outlet

The router-outlet directive in Angular acts as a placeholder where the router should display the component for the current route. It's like a dynamic component loader controlled by the router. When you navigate between routes, Angular removes the previous component and inserts the new component in place of the router-outlet. This allows you to switch between different parts of your application without a full page reload. Here's a simple example: Let's say you have two routes: /home and /about. Both routes are tied to two separate components: HomeComponent and AboutComponent. When a user navigates to /home, the HomeComponent will be loaded and displayed where the router-outlet is located in your main app template. When the user navigates to /about, the HomeComponent will be removed, and the AboutComponent will be loaded and displayed in the same spot.
